# Nervous System Configurations: Three States

## Definition

The nervous system organizes itself into distinct configurations, each with different availability, aliveness, and relational capacity.

## The Three Configurations

### 1. Hypervigilance (Shame-Organized)
- **State:** Nervous system is mobilized, alert, defended
- **Energy pattern:** High activation, consumed by tracking perception
- **Aliveness:** Present but anxious, performing rather than authentic
- **Availability:** Limited—energy goes to management and defense
- **Marker:** Watchfulness, reactivity, impression-management

**When it occurs:** When someone is trying to manage how they're perceived; when shame is active

### 2. Shutdown (Resignation-Organized)
- **State:** Nervous system is dorsal vagal activated, withdrawn
- **Energy pattern:** Low activation, collapsed, numb
- **Aliveness:** Absent—the person is disconnected from feeling and engagement
- **Availability:** None—the nervous system has decided "nothing is possible"
- **Marker:** Flatness, absence, disconnection, numbness

**When it occurs:** When someone has given up on metabolizing; when resignation has set in

### 3. Regulated Aliveness (Authentic Presence)
- **State:** Nervous system is regulated but engaged, available
- **Energy pattern:** Balanced activation, responsive but not defensive
- **Aliveness:** Present and engaged with what's actually happening
- **Availability:** Full—energy available for connection, learning, adaptation
- **Marker:** Settledness, responsiveness, authenticity, presence

**When it occurs:** When shame has been metabolized and the person can simply be as they are

## Clinical Application

### The Therapeutic Movement

Therapy can be understood partly as helping people move through these configurations toward regulated aliveness:

**From hypervigilance (shame-driven defense):**
- Reduce the need to manage perception
- Metabolize shame about limitations
- Find safety in authenticity rather than performance

**From shutdown (resignation):**
- Restore sense of possibility
- Re-engage the nervous system with engagement and values
- Move from "nothing is possible" to "something is possible"

**Toward regulated aliveness:**
- Nervous system learns it's safe to be authentic
- Energy becomes available for genuine connection and growth
- Person can bring their whole self to relationships

### The Role of Relationship

The therapist's own nervous system configuration is crucial:

- **Therapist in hypervigilance** → Client senses defense and closes
- **Therapist in shutdown** → Client loses hope and engagement
- **Therapist in regulated aliveness** → Client's nervous system can relax toward authenticity
