# Research Queue

## Priority 1: Ground "Narrow Window of Awareness"

### Predictive Processing / Predictive Coding
**Why**: Directly addresses how the brain creates "meaning" from limited data
**Key authors**: Karl Friston, Andy Clark, Anil Seth
**Search terms**: "predictive processing psychology", "free energy principle", "predictive coding perception"
**Questions to answer**:
- How does the brain construct predictions from sensory data?
- What happens when predictions conflict with reality?
- How does this relate to belief formation?

### Constructivist Epistemology
**Why**: Philosophical grounding for "meaning is constructed, not discovered"
**Key authors**: Ernst von Glasersfeld, Jean Piaget, George Kelly
**Search terms**: "constructivism psychology", "personal construct theory", "radical constructivism"
**Questions to answer**:
- What's the relationship between constructed meaning and "truth"?
- How do constructivists handle the question of shared reality?

### Existentialist Psychology
**Why**: Addresses meaning-making as central human concern
**Key authors**: Irvin Yalom, Viktor Frankl, Rollo May
**Search terms**: "existential psychotherapy", "meaning-making therapy", "logotherapy"
**Questions to answer**:
- How does existentialism frame the "necessity" of meaning?
- What's the relationship between meaning and suffering?

## Priority 2: Necessary vs. Unnecessary Pain

### Terror Management Theory
**Why**: Addresses how awareness of mortality drives meaning-making (and suffering)
**Key authors**: Sheldon Solomon, Jeff Greenberg, Tom Pyszczynski
**Search terms**: "terror management theory", "mortality salience", "worldview defense"
**Questions to answer**:
- What pain is "built in" to human awareness?
- Can awareness of death be transformed rather than defended against?

### Buddhist Psychology (Secular)
**Why**: Sophisticated framework for distinguishing suffering types
**Key authors**: Mark Epstein, Tara Brach, Jack Kornfield
**Search terms**: "dukkha psychology", "first and second arrow", "Buddhist psychotherapy"
**Questions to answer**:
- What's the difference between pain and suffering?
- What's "added" suffering vs. unavoidable pain?

## Priority 3: Neurodivergence & The Window

### ADHD and Perception
**Why**: Different attentional window — not narrower, differently shaped?
**Key authors**: Russell Barkley, Edward Hallowell, Thomas Brown
**Search terms**: "ADHD perception", "ADHD attention differences", "ADHD and meaning-making"
**Questions to answer**:
- How does ADHD change what gets "noticed" and therefore made into meaning?
- Is the ADHD window wider in some dimensions, narrower in others?

### Autism and Meaning-Making
**Why**: Different pattern-detection, different meaning construction
**Key authors**: Simon Baron-Cohen, Temple Grandin, Damian Milton
**Search terms**: "autism perception", "monotropism", "double empathy problem"
**Questions to answer**:
- How does autistic perception differ in meaning-construction?
- What's the relationship between pattern-detection style and worldview?
